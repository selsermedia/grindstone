Ontario Fly Fishing

Southern Ontario and its northern reaches into Georgian Bay boast some of the finest and most diverse fly fishing in the country, if not the world. The fly fishing opportunities are a sportsman’s dream and despite being in proximity to the largest population in the country, you can still find solitude if you are willing to work just a bit harder than the rest. The Saugeen and Grand River typify the diversity of the fishery in this region. Both systems are big and both hold cold, cool and warm water species. It is possible to fish either of these two rivers for the entire season without ever getting bored as resident trout, steelhead, bass, muskie, pike and salmon inhabit them at some point in the season.

The Ontario Fishery

With its many lakes, rivers and streams, easy access and a spectacular variety of game fishing opportunities, Southern Ontario is a Mecca for fly fishers. Take a closer look at Ontario's fishery and learn about the species we target.

Ontario Rivers

With excellent water quality and a variety of conditions, the rivers and streams of southern and south central Ontario are home to a vast insect population and healthy, hard-fighting fish. Our experienced guides will put you on a productive Ontario river that meets your requirements. Read more about Ontario rivers.
